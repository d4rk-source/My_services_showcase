# Readiness Diary - Gambling

## How I Approached It

I focused on the conditions that make a gambling review meaningful: fairness, payout safety, and who can change the rules. I started with the review blockers, then checked whether the codebase was presented in a way auditors could move through quickly.

## What I Did

- Identified the trust model for randomness and market resolution
- Separated structural concerns from gameplay details
- Built a reviewer-friendly remediation queue
- Kept the protocol simple enough that the review story is easy to follow

## Methodology Notes

- I treated the readiness pass as a pre-audit filter, not a full audit.
- I focused on documentation quality, issue clarity, and fix verification planning.
- The goal was to make the next external review cheaper and cleaner.

## Why This Helps a Client

Good readiness work lowers the chance of wasting audit time on avoidable issues. It makes the protocol easier to review and harder to misunderstand.
